---
title: "Quickstart"
description: "Make your first voice-dictated entry in under two minutes."
---

This guide assumes you have already [installed Voice Input](/installation) and granted the required permissions. By the end you will have dictated text into a real app using the Fn key.

<Tip>
  If this is your first time using voice dictation, try it in a low-stakes app like **TextEdit** or the macOS **Notes** app. It is a forgiving environment for getting comfortable with the hold-speak-release rhythm before using Voice Input in your everyday workflow.
</Tip>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the Voice Input icon in the menu bar">
    After launching Voice Input, look for its icon on the right side of your menu bar. Because Voice Input runs as a menu-bar-only app (no Dock icon), the menu bar icon is your main control panel.

    Click the icon to open the menu. You will see options for language selection, LLM Refinement, and Quit.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select your preferred language">
    From the menu bar menu, hover over the language list and choose the language you want to dictate in. Voice Input supports:

    - English
    - Simplified Chinese (zh-CN) — the default
    - Traditional Chinese (zh-TW)
    - Japanese
    - Korean

    Your selection is saved automatically and persists across relaunches.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click into any text field">
    Open any app that accepts text input — a browser address bar, a chat window, a notes app, a code editor — and click to focus the input field where you want your text to appear.

    Voice Input injects text into whatever field has keyboard focus at the moment you release the Fn key, so make sure the right field is active before you start recording.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Hold the Fn key and speak">
    Press and hold the **Fn** key (bottom-left of most Mac keyboards). A floating capsule window appears at the bottom of your screen containing:

    - A **5-bar waveform** that responds to your voice in real time — louder speech produces taller bars
    - A **live transcription preview** that updates as you speak

    Speak naturally. You do not need to pause between words or use special commands.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Release the Fn key to inject your text">
    When you have finished speaking, release the **Fn** key. Voice Input:

    1. Finalizes the transcription
    2. Briefly switches to an ASCII input source if a CJK input method is active (to prevent paste interception)
    3. Copies the text to the clipboard and simulates **Cmd+V** to paste it
    4. Restores your original clipboard contents

    The text appears in the focused input field within a moment. The floating capsule window fades out automatically.
  </Step>

  <Step title="(Optional) Enable LLM refinement for better accuracy">
    If you frequently dictate mixed-language content (for example, English technical terms inside Chinese sentences) or notice recurring transcription errors, turn on LLM Refinement.

    1. Click the Voice Input menu bar icon
    2. Open **LLM Refinement → Settings**
    3. Enter your **API Base URL**, **API Key**, and **Model** for any OpenAI-compatible provider
    4. Click **Save**, then enable the **LLM Refinement** toggle

    When enabled, the floating window shows a **Refining...** status after you release Fn, and the corrected text is injected once the model responds. The LLM is intentionally conservative — it only fixes clear errors and never rewrites content that appears correct.
  </Step>
</Steps>
